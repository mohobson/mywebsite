Secret Santa

Overview

On monthxx dayxx, I made my first trade on the stock market (1 share of Bank of America at $25). Since then, I developed trading strategies that I use on a weekly basis. As I entered the workforce in 2019, I found myself stretching to perform all of the market research I wanted to conduct for short-term trades. So, I began developing this program to buy back the time I lost from working full-time. This is my favorite personal project because it has enabled me to continue to do two things I love: trading and programming. The program is under continuous development and has been over the last couple years as new ideas and needs pop up.

SMAs

Simple technical analysis has been rewarding for me. Using simple moving averages (SMAs), I can identify breakouts and trends for different stocks. I could spend hours opening up charts in ThinkorSwim, wondering where to start, but that is time consuming. Using criteria, I have automated this process in a personalized way using Robinhood's API. With the click of a button, this program will create an Excel sheet and a series of charts with tickers experiencing breakouts or trends. This information is then emailed to me so I can conduct further research at a much better starting point.

CSPs

I also have various strategies for options trading. My most common option trading strategy is selling cash secured puts (CSPs). Selling options is a risky strategy, and should never be done without extensive research (TRUST ME!!). A major module within this program is called CSP.py. It is capable of filtering out the riskier option contracts (low volume, low chance of profit, etc.), providing me with a starting point for further research in the CSP tab of the Excel sheet.

Positions

Finally, this program provides a tab that displays my current stock equity, option equity, and some additional information from my account on Robinhood.
